Food and Bites

Maximillien

Maximillien Maximilien is a romantic French bistro in Seattle’s Pike Place Market, known for its Parisian ambiance, locally sourced dishes, and stunning views of Puget Sound. It offers cozy indoor dining, seasonal patio igloos, and a menu featuring French classics like duck confit, seafood, and crème brûlée.

Sushi Kashiba

Sushi Kashiba Sushi Kashiba is an acclaimed Edomae-style sushi restaurant near Pike Place Market, led by legendary chef Shiro Kashiba, a protégé of Jiro Ono. Known for its elegant omakase experience, it features seasonal Pacific Northwest seafood, xpertly crafted nigiri, and a signature tamago dessert.

Lonely Siren

Lonely Siren is a cozy, Portuguese-inspired tapas and cocktail bar tucked into Pike Place Market, offering inventive small plates like grilled sardines, porco à alentejana, batatas bravas, and salt‑cod croquettes alongside uniquely crafted drinks such as ginja cherry liqueur and porto tônico. With its moody, plant-filled greenhouse space, walk-in-friendly vibe, and warm ambiance, it's become a standout spot for spontaneous gatherings or late-night drinks in Seattle .

Le Painer

Le Panier is a beloved French bakery and pâtisserie in Pike Place Market, serving classic freshly baked baguettes, viennoiseries (croissants, pain au chocolat, macarons), and savory tartlets since 1983 under French-trained bakers Kristi Drake and Thierry Mougin 

Seattle Dumpling co.

Seattle Dumpling Co. Dumpling Co. is a casual, counter‑style dumpling and noodle spot tucked into Pike Place Market, serving handmade Chinese comfort food like pork-chive, shrimp, and xiaolongbao dumplings, as well as dan dan and beef noodle soups. Rated around 4.2–4.5 stars, it offers quick service with modest seating for walk‑in diners, and is appreciated for its flavorful offerings though sometimes a bit cramped .
